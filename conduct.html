Code of Conduct

This Code of Conduct exists to keep our community safe, welcoming, and creatively vibrant. It applies to all spaces where the Fellowship gathers—in game, on voice/chat, and here on the website. There are two pillars: In‑Character (IC) Conduct, which governs how characters behave within the story, and Out‑of‑Character (OOC) Conduct, which governs how players treat one another.

In‑Character (IC) Conduct

Consent & Collaboration
  • No god‑modding: Do not control another player’s character, actions, emotions, or outcomes.
  • No meta‑gaming: Keep OOC knowledge out of IC decisions.
  • Check‑ins: If a scene escalates (violence, fear, grief), do quick OOC check‑ins to confirm comfort.
  • Retcons: If consent was unclear, a brief retcon is acceptable with agreement from all involved.
Power, Peril, and Stakes
  • Earned impact: High‑impact actions (injury, captivity, public defamation) require prior OOC consent.
  • Proportionality: Match force and consequence to the scene; avoid one‑sided “win buttons.”
  • DM rulings: When a DM is present, their call stands for that scene; follow‑up after for feedback.
Tone & Lore Alignment
  • Setting fit: Keep character beats and magic within the shared tone and established lore guides.
  • Continuity: Respect ongoing arcs and prior outcomes; use session recaps to stay aligned.

Out‑of‑Character (OOC) Conduct

Respect & Safety
  • Administrative Approach: We don't have interest in heavily policing people's conduct. If you seem unable to adhere to our Code of Conduct, then it's clear we're not a good match for you. We don't want to force anyone to change themselves to fit our model; there are so many guilds that may be a better fit, and we want everyone to be comfortable in their social environment.
  • Zero harassment: No personal attacks, slurs, or targeted disruption. No doxxing or threats.
  • Boundaries: Honor stated content boundaries immediately, without debate. This includes in social chats.
  • Chat Etiquette: While dark humor and the like are not prohibited, the expectation is for it to punch upwards, instead of down.
  • Private matters: Do not share private logs, DMs, or personal information without consent.
Mature Content
  • ERP: What individual players do in their own time is their own business, but decisions on violations here are with made regard to how public behavior reflects on the guild.
  • Profiles: As above, what individual players put in their profile is their own business, but it should not violate any other part of our code of conduct.
  • Links: Links to things like f-lists and explicit art (or anything that may violate the WoW social policy and ToS) should be made on available on request and not openly provided.
Voice, Chat, and Spoilers
  • Comms: Keep voice comms clear during events; reserve back‑channel chat for non‑blocking notes.
  • Chat: We're not going to police conversations save for clear cut violations of the code of conduct. If you have to be repeatedly warned about behavior in chat, though, it's a simple matter of us not being the right fit for you.
  • Spoilers: Use spoiler tags or give a heads‑up for major story info from recent content.
  • Disagreements: Take heated topics to DMs or a moderator; de‑escalate in public channels.
Inclusivity
  • We welcome players of all backgrounds. We do not tolerate discrimination based on identity or circumstance.
  • Accessibility matters: be patient with new or returning players; teach, don’t gatekeep.

Enforcement & Reporting

If you experience or witness a violation, contact an officer or Ansinosth. We will handle reports promptly, fairly, and with discretion. Possible actions include: clarification, mediated conversation, temporary mute/suspension, or removal from the community.

  • How to report: DM a moderator as soon as possible.
  • Evidence helps: Screenshots/logs make it easier to review and resolve.
  • Appeals: You may request a post‑decision review by the council.

Event Participation

  • Try to be respectful of the DM's time; we don't demand attendance, but chronically late members who interrupt and disrupt ongoing RP will be disinvited.
  • Have your UI/addons and audio ready before start; observe push‑to‑talk etiquette in voice.
  • Follow the event lead’s direction and the scene’s safety tools.Take initiative to get creative, but don't derail.

Acknowledgement

By participating in Fellowship spaces, you agree to abide by this Code of Conduct. We update policies as needed to support a healthy, creative environment.
